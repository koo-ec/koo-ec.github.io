# Research

My research sits at the intersection of AI safety, dependability, explainability, and engineering methods for safety-critical systems. I am especially interested in assurance workflows that make AI systems inspectable, monitorable, and useful in real-world critical domains.

## Research Themes

### Responsible and Trustworthy AI

I develop techniques for assessing and improving the trustworthiness of AI systems, with emphasis on responsible deployment, safety monitoring, fairness, uncertainty, and evidence that can support decisions in critical settings.

### LLM and Multimodal AI Safety

Recent work focuses on domain-specific safety monitoring for large language models, multimodal AI alignment, document-heavy decision workflows, and human-agent collaboration for specifying safety and alignment objectives.

### Explainable AI

I work on robust, model-agnostic explainability methods that help users understand why models behave as they do. This includes SMILE/XWhy and explanation workflows for safety-critical ML applications.

### Dependability of Autonomous Systems

I study model-based safety, runtime assurance, fault diagnosis, and digital-twin techniques for autonomous systems, drones, multi-robot systems, and industrial AI applications.

## Methods

| Method family | Typical use |
| --- | --- |
| Statistical safety monitoring | Detecting unsafe or uncertain ML behavior at runtime. |
| Explainable AI | Producing interpretable evidence for model behavior. |
| Model-based safety engineering | Connecting architectures, hazards, faults, and runtime assurance. |
| Digital twins | Evaluating behavior and dependability under realistic operating conditions. |
| Human-agent workflows | Supporting collaborative specification, review, and assurance of AI behavior. |
